## Executive Summary
- An oil spike on the Iran escalation left equities flat to lower; Bitcoin absorbed the same shock and finished ahead of both major indices for a second week.
- The first downside core inflation print in five months lands a week before the FOMC meets, with policy still holding firmly restrictive above it.
- The 10-year yield is pressing back toward its highs while the dollar rests; the yield-and-dollar ceiling over this market has not lifted.
- The recovery has carried price into the decision zone: the **Short-Term Holder Cost Basis at $69K** overhead, the market's heaviest demand shelf just beneath.
- Near-price supply is tilting back toward support, exchange inflows keep fading, and the profit-taking fuel a rejection would need has not yet built.
- Accumulation has narrowed to the high-conviction 1k-10k BTC cohort; breadth is the missing on-chain ingredient.
- Off-chain, the confirmation this report has tracked for weeks arrived: ETF flows flipped positive, price sits above max pain, and hedging has collapsed to its cheapest in months.
- Altcoins are slipping against Bitcoin again, capital concentrating in the leader, and the Market Compass reads the same way: repairing, still risk-off.

## Macro — Shockproof Against the Oil Spike
WTI crude spiked on the Iran escalation, the kind of exogenous shock that usually drags the whole risk complex lower; equities behaved accordingly (S&P finished lower, European stocks flat). Bitcoin took the same shock and kept climbing, finishing ahead of both indices for the second week running. A market that rallies through bad news is one where the marginal seller is spent — the resilience itself is the signal.

## Macro — Inflation Finally Bends
Core inflation delivered its first downside print in five months, the closest to target since the post-2022 spike. One print is not a trend, but the FOMC meets next week with the funds rate still holding more than a full point above core inflation — the definition of restrictive policy. A signal that cuts are back on the table would release one of the macro brakes.

## Macro — Yields Push, the Dollar Rests
The 10-year Treasury yield has climbed back within reach of its recent highs (long-end pressure on every risk asset), while the dollar settled far below its winter extreme. The ceiling that has capped this cycle's advances: a 10-year yield above **4.45%** and a dollar index above **99**. The dollar side has nearly cleared; the yield side has not. A durable break below both marks = the macro release valve.

## On-chain — Arrival at the Decision Zone
Overhead sits the **Short-Term Holder Cost Basis near $69K**, the break-even of the past five months of buyers. Just beneath spot sits the **heaviest demand shelf on the chart, roughly a tenth of supply centred on $63K**, where the median coin last changed hands. The **Realized Price floor waits far below**. The first wall's asymmetry: approached from below in a downtrend, the break-even of recent buyers acts as a rejection zone (holders about to be made whole sell). Above it, the profile thins into an **air pocket all the way to the $84K wall**. Reclaim the level → little in the way of a fast repricing; fail there → the shelf below becomes the test.

## On-chain — Support Gathers Into the Test
Proximity-weighted cost basis data splits nearby coins into support below spot and resistance above; the **support share has just overtaken its counterpart**, a regime resistance-led since spring on the verge of flipping. A rejection needs sellers, and that fuel is not loaded: the classic trigger arrives when **STH Supply in Profit pushes through 54%** (still well short), and **STH-SOPR is steadying at its break-even line** rather than rolling over. Recent buyers neither euphoric nor exiting.

## On-chain — Sell-Side Steps Back
**Exchange Net Position Change** swelled into a heavy inflow wave through early June as the market broke down (latent sell-side supply arriving), then faded week after week and now runs at a fraction of its peak. Fading inflows without dominant outflows = a neutral regime. The confirmation to watch: a flip to **sustained net outflows** — the on-chain follow-through the ETF channel has just begun to supply.

## On-chain — Conviction Without Breadth
June's rebound was bought broadly (Accumulation Trend Score lit across every wallet cohort into the lows). The past two weeks: the bid narrowed to the **1k-10k BTC cohort** (wallets that front-run durable turns), while mid-tier cohorts rotated back to distribution. Breadth returning on the next push higher = the tell that separates a squeeze from a trend.

## Off-chain — The Redemption Streak Breaks
**US Spot ETF Flows have flipped positive**, the first sustained stretch of net buying since the June redemption wave. It converts a derivatives-led recovery into one with a spot bid behind it. Persistence, not size, is what to watch.

## Off-chain — Above the Pin
**Max pain** capped this market from above all spring; Bitcoin now trades just above the aggregate level (it sat far beneath at the February depths). Reclaiming the pin historically accompanies a friendlier options regime; holding above into the coming expiries would put dealer hedging flows to work dampening moves rather than chasing them — pin from ceiling into anchor.

## Off-chain — Hedges Off, Shorts Fold
The **one-week 25-Delta Skew has collapsed to its cheapest in months** — downside protection abandoned fastest at the front of the curve, and the move runs through the one-month tenor as well. The **Options Open Interest Put/Call Ratio has fallen to its lowest of the year**, the volume ratio has halved from its June hedging spike, and **perpetual funding has held below its neutral line every day of the past month**. The optimism comes from hedges being lifted, not fresh leverage — squeezes built on that mix retrace less violently than funding-driven ones.

## Off-chain — Alts Bleed, Bitcoin Leads
Every altcoin tier has fallen against Bitcoin for years, flattening this spring into the most constructive altcoin floor of the downtrend; over the past week the slide resumed, small caps giving ground against BTC as the market pushed higher. Bitcoin-led advances concentrate capital in the asset with the deepest bid before strength spills outward; alt-led strength this early = froth. The floor holding while Bitcoin leads is the sequence a durable recovery wants.

## The Compass Agrees
Glassnode's Market Compass: **Cycle Position** reads deep-cycle accumulation territory (ticked higher) alongside **Investor Behaviour** (capitulation fading). **Derivatives** gave back some ground but only after recovering from washed-out toward balanced (deleveraged, not frothy). Composite still reads **Risk-Off** — lenses repairing inside a defensive regime is what an early turn looks like; it flips constructive with breadth and follow-through, not with one squeeze.

## Conclusion
Still a **bear-market rally** until proven otherwise. The squeeze did all it can (hedges off, shorts closed, funding calm, ETF channel flipped from drag to bid) but has not cleared the overhead. Bitcoin enters the week **beneath the Short-Term Holder Cost Basis at $69K**, with an air pocket beyond it and the **$84K wall** above; beneath sit the **$63K demand shelf** and a support regime still forming. A decisive **reclaim of $69K on persistent spot inflows opens the pocket above**; **rejection there, with exchange inflows returning, sends the market back to the shelf.**
